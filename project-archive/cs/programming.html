Total Recall (1987) Revisted
Gretchen Bender: So Much Deathless
Red Bull Arts New York, Curator of Public Programs, 2019
A live score with Stuart Argabright.

On May 3, composer Stuart Argabright will return to his score for Bender’s landmark work Total Recall (1987), accompanying the work of electronic theater with an added intro, outro, and expanded composition, intimately staged within the installation of Bender’s proscenium of screens. A one-night only event to experience Total Recall revisited, Argabright pulls from his archive of cassette tapes originally used in 1987 to construct the work’s expansive soundscape, which includes audio samples from car, soda, and early computer commercials, as well as the videos appropriated by Bender herself, such as Oliver Stone’s movie Salvador (1986) and commercials for the US Army Reserve.

IMAGE HAZE: A Tribute to Gretchen Bender’s So Much Deathless
Red Bull Arts New York, Curator of Public Programs, 2019
Two live audio-visual performances featuring Stuart Argabright and Black Rain, Philip Vanderhyden, and Kaitlyn Aurelia Smith. Co-presented with ISSUE Project Room.

Held on Saturday, May 18, the two live audio-visual performances have been organized in response to Bender’s unfinished work of media theater, which was to be entitled So Much Deathless. In writing on Bender’s earlier Total Recall (1987), the critic Jonathan Crary compared her electrifying performance to the narcotic “image haze” of 80s television, stating that Bender “outlines survival tactics for artists to maintain a mobile position at the boundaries of mass culture.” These performances follow in Bender’s footsteps, and continue to sketch survival tactics for our evolving media and cultural landscape.

GLOWING CREATION: A Rammellzee-inspired Opera
RAMMELLZEE: Racing for Thunder
Red Bull Arts New York, Curator of Public Programs, 2018
Artist-led workshops with Kool Koor, Tau Lewis, and Gio Escobar from Standing on the Corner. Organized in collaboration with the Abrons Arts Center.

Over the course of six weeks—inspired by Rammellzee’s unique brand of performance art and ornate costumery—a group of students, under the tutelage of visiting artists, designed personalized Tyvek suits and masks, created musical tracks, and developed singular performances featuring their own interpretive break-dancing and choreography. Visiting artists included Kool Koor, Tau Lewis, and Standing on the Corner’s Gio Escobar.
